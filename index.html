Our Beloved Patron Saints

St. John Berchmans

A Jesuit seminarian known for holiness in daily life and devotion to duty. Patron of altar servers and students.

St. Tarcisius

A young Roman martyr who died protecting the Eucharist. Patron of first communicants and altar servers.

St. Dominic Savio

A student of St. John Bosco known for his purity and strong devotion. Inspired young people to serve God faithfully.

St. Pedro Calungsod

A young Filipino catechist and altar server who assisted Jesuit missionaries in Guam. Martyred at age 17 in 1672 for defending the faith. Canonized in 2012, he is honored as a patron of altar servers, youth, and catechists.

St. Anthony Mary Zaccaria


Born in 1502 in Cremona, Italy, St. Anthony Mary Zaccaria founded the Barnabites and promoted spiritual renewal among clergy and laity. His zeal for the Eucharist and reform of the Church made him a model of priestly service and devotion. He died at 36, leaving a legacy of faith and discipline.

The Ministry of Altar Servers under the St. Anthony Mary Zaccaria Parish is composed of dedicated young men serving faithfully at the altar. They uphold discipline, humility, and unity in every liturgical service.
